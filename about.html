About Me

Urban researcher and geospatial analyst exploring cities, systems, and the interconnections that shape our world.

Background

I hold an MSc in International City Planning from UCL (University College London), where I developed expertise in urban systems, planning theory, and policy analysis. My undergraduate degree was a BA in International Relations from SOAS (School of Oriental and African Studies), which provided me with a foundation in global systems thinking and cross-cultural understanding.

This unique combination of training allows me to approach urban challenges through both technical planning frameworks and broader geopolitical contexts.

Professional Experience

I have contributed to research for the 2026 edition of the Happy City Index, an ambitious project that measures urban well-being across multiple dimensions including health, education, social connections, and environmental quality. This work deepened my understanding of how cities function as complex systems and how measurable indicators can reveal what truly matters for urban resilience and quality of life.

Current Focus

After gaining experience through collaborative research projects, I am now focusing on creating my own analytical outputs and original research. I'm driven by the desire to generate independent insights about how urban systems work, how environmental corridors support city life, and how grassroots initiatives shape more resilient communities.

My work combines rigorous geospatial analysis, mapping methodologies, and qualitative research to explore questions that matter for urban futures. I'm particularly interested in mapping the systems we often take for granted and revealing the hidden networks that hold our cities together.

Interests and Approaches

  • Environmental networks and green infrastructure
  • Geospatial analysis and mapping
  • Community-led urban initiatives
  • Systems thinking and complexity in cities
  • Data visualization for urban understanding
  • Participatory research methodologies
